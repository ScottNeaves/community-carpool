About

Socially responsible and committed, Community Carpool's ride team is devoted to transforming transportation for all our children. Community Carpool was purpose-built by creative and passionate people to solve the transportation problems facing our most vulnerable passengers.

Mark Tabakin

As a student of UPenn's Education Entrepreneurship Master’s program and a father of four, Mark saw an opportunity to found a company that would ease transportation pains for both parents and educational instutions, and build trust among neighbors at the same time. Mark brings to the table a strong business acumen and a host of valuable relationships with members of the education community, developed over the course of a successful career as an attorney in the field of education.

Scott Neaves

Scott is a product-minded software developer tackling the tech side of Community Carpool. With software development experience in enterprise, consulting, and startup environments, Scott is well prepared to tackle all of the technical challenges associated with providing safe, flexible tranportation to families and communities around the country.

Jacob Sack

Jacob is a Director at Oxeon Partners, a Healthcare Growth Services firm committed to Making People Healthier. He works with entrepreneurs and more established businesses in the Healthcare IT and Services sector to improve individual and community health. Before joining Oxeon, Jacob worked at the Edna McConnell Clark Foundation (EMCF) – a social venture investment firm – as a Senior Portfolio Associate. Jacob caught the social venture bug during his prior experience as a program and business development manager at Public/Private Ventures, a policy and research organization committed to understanding what works (and what doesn’t!) to improve the lives of the most vulnerable young people and families.

When he’s not in the office, or working at odd hours with his Community Carpool co-founder, you’re likely to find Jacob running through NYC. He was deeply inspired by Christopher McDougall’s “Born to Run” – or traveling to be with friends and family. Sack earned a bachelor’s degree in government and legal studies from Bowdoin College and his Master’s in Education Entrepreneurship in a joint School of Education/Wharton program at Penn.
